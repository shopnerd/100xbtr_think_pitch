The problem

Your team's thinking is all over the place.

Slack threads, Figma jams, Notion docs, calls nobody recorded, decisions made on a walk. The structure of what you figured out together disappears the moment it's captured. Everyone reconstructs their own version.

  • Chatthreads bury the reasoning behind decisions.
  • Docsgo stale the moment they're written.
  • Whiteboardsbecome a photo and a vague memory.
  • AI toolssit outside the work, as chat sidebars.
How it works

One canvas. One document.
Same underlying graph.

Build the thinking on a shared visual canvas. The document is a live, always-current page rendered from the canvas — decisions with reasoning, actions with owners, questions still open, references indexed. Share a link; the thinking is the record.

Private

The canvas

Where the team thinks. Typed nodes for ideas, decisions, questions, actions, references. Typed wires for supports, blocks, depends-on, spawned. Groups cluster concepts. Claude reads the whole graph, can suggest connections, flag what's missing, and build structure while you watch. Real-time, multiplayer, auth-gated.

Public or private

The document

What walks out the door. Always current. Auto-generated from the canvas state. Investors see the decision history. New teammates read it on day one. Stakeholders get a link, not a deck. No separate write-up step — the act of thinking is the act of documenting.
